\title{A Jovian Decametric Occurrence Census from Juno/Waves: Proximity Dominates, and the
Canonical Io Source Regions Persist --- Reduced --- at the Orbital Vantage}
\author[0009-0008-3289-4447]{Joseph Barbere}
\affiliation{Independent researcher}

\begin{abstract}
Ground-based Jovian decametric (DAM) emission is famously organised in the plane of observer
central meridian longitude (CML) and Io orbital phase into the Io-A/B/C/D source regions
\citep{bigg1964,carr1983,marques2017}. The public Juno/Waves calibrated flux-density dataset
\citep{louis2021} allows the same census \emph{from orbit}: we build an occurrence map over one
Juno orbit (2017 February; \jdNbins\ 15-s bins, \jdNact\ active) using the dataset's shipped
per-channel background statistics for detection, JPL Horizons for the sub-Juno System~III CML
(the naive IAU rotation formula errs by up to $\sim$40$^\circ$ at Juno, verified), and the Lieske
ephemeris for Io phase \citep{lieske1987,archinal2018}. On a synthetic orbit with the canonical
Io boxes injected the census recovers the enhancement within cell-edge smearing (and the
synthetic round-trip is a machinery check only --- an earlier Io-phase convention error, caught
in science review, passed it while displacing every box; the convention is now the standard
$\Phi_{Io}=\mathrm{CML}+180^\circ-\Lambda_{Io}$ of Bigg 1964 / Carr et al.\ 1983). On the real month, two results: (i)
\emph{proximity dominates} --- the DAM-band duty cycle is \jdActNear\% in the near half of the
orbit versus \jdActFar\% in the far half, a factor $\sim$25 driven by Juno--Jupiter distance,
not by any rotational clock; and (ii) in the canonical (CML, $\Phi_{Io}$) boxes the occurrence contrast is \jdContrast\
(full month; \jdContrastFar\ in the far half alone) --- Io organisation genuinely persists at
the orbital vantage, but reduced relative to the strong ground-based contrasts, as expected if
part of the canonical structure encodes beaming toward a distant near-equatorial observer. One
month cannot separate vantage physics from exposure; the multi-orbit extension (the same code
over the 2016--2023 v02 archive) is the scoped follow-on.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Io's control of Jovian DAM \citep{bigg1964} is the textbook example of a satellite--magnetosphere
interaction, and the (CML, Io-phase) occurrence map with its A/B/C/D regions \citep{carr1983,
marques2017} is its canonical representation --- built from decades of \emph{terrestrial}
observations, i.e.\ from a fixed, distant, near-equatorial vantage. Juno's polar, eccentric orbit
breaks every one of those assumptions. The 2025 public release of the calibrated Juno/Waves
flux densities \citep{louis2021} (daily CDFs to 40.5~MHz with per-channel background and sigma)
makes the from-orbit census reproducible with public data alone.

\section{Method}
Each daily CDF is binned to 15~s; a bin is DAM-active when $\ge$10\% of the 3--40.5~MHz channels
exceed the shipped $\mathrm{Background}+5\Sigma$. The absolute scale (V$^2$m$^{-2}$Hz$^{-1}$)
never enters --- an occurrence census needs only detection against the dataset's own statistics.
The sub-Juno CML comes from Horizons (\code{PDObsLon}, observer \code{500@-61}) at 15-min
sampling, unwrap-interpolated; Io phase is the Lieske mean longitude minus CML
\citep{lieske1987}. Occurrence maps carry per-cell exposure, and cells visited $<$3 times are
masked --- a single orbit does not sample the plane uniformly, and hiding that would manufacture
structure. The synthetic recover-a-known drives the same code with real CML/Io rates and
Bernoulli activity concentrated in the canonical boxes: injected contrast 8.75, recovered 7.2.

\section{Results and honest limits}
Figure~\ref{fig:jd} shows the month's map. The near/far duty-cycle split
(\jdActNear\%/\jdActFar\%) says the strongest organising variable from orbit is simply distance
--- at perijove Juno sits close to (at times within) the source regions and the band is active
almost continuously, while in the far half the duty cycle drops to Earth-like rarity. The
canonical Io boxes organise the residual at contrast \jdContrast\ (full month; \jdContrastFar\
far-half; \jdCells\ cells used) --- present, but well below ground-based values. Honest limits: (i) one orbit --- (CML, $\Phi_{Io}$) exposure is uneven;
(ii) the fixed 10\%-of-band activity criterion is one choice among several (it suppresses
narrowband interference but dilutes narrowband DAM); (iii) proximity and vantage effects are not
modelled, only demonstrated; (iv) the census does not separate Io-DAM from non-Io-DAM or
kilometric leakage below 3~MHz; (v) the reduced-contrast reading is a consistency statement, not a proof --- the multi-orbit
v02 census with distance-resolved maps is the test; and the review-caught convention error is
disclosed above because a synthetic round-trip cannot validate frame conventions, only machinery.
