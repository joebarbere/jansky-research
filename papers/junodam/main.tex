\title{A Multi-Orbit Jovian Decametric Occurrence Census from Juno/Waves: The Range-Occurrence
Trend Is an Inverse-Square Detection Effect, and the Earth-Canonical Io Source Regions Do Not
Coherently Organise the Orbital Vantage}
\author[0009-0008-3289-4447]{Joseph Barbere}
\affiliation{Independent researcher}

\begin{abstract}
Ground-based Jovian decametric (DAM) emission is famously organised in the plane of observer
central meridian longitude (CML) and Io orbital phase into the Io-A/B/C/D source regions
\citep{bigg1964,carr1983,marques2017}. The public Juno/Waves calibrated flux-density dataset
\citep{louis2021} enables the same census \emph{from orbit}: we build an occurrence map over seven
months spanning six orbital configurations (2016--2019; \jdNbins\ 15-s bins, \jdNact\
active), using the dataset's per-channel background statistics for detection, Horizons for the
sub-Juno CML, and the Lieske ephemeris for Io phase \citep{lieske1987}. A synthetic round-trip
with the Io boxes injected recovers them (a machinery check only; a review-caught Io-phase
convention error is corrected to the Bigg 1964 / Carr et al.\ 1983 standard). On the
7-month aggregate, two results. (i) \emph{The raw proximity trend is almost entirely detection
sensitivity}: the DAM-band duty cycle falls from \jdAqA\% in the nearest Juno--Jupiter range
quartile ($\sim$\jdRangeNear~$R_J$) to \jdAqD\% in the farthest ($\sim$\jdRangeFar~$R_J$), a raw
factor $\sim$\jdNearFarRaw, but a $1/r^2$ sensitivity null (distance-correct each bin's DAM SNR and
re-threshold) collapses it to \jdNearFarCorr --- the apparent proximity ``law'' is a
threshold-amplified inverse-square visibility effect, not an $\sim$180$\times$ intrinsic rise, and
the residual is an upper bound entangled with viewing geometry. (ii) \emph{The Earth-canonical
(CML, $\Phi_{Io}$) boxes do not coherently organise the orbital-vantage occurrence}: the aggregate
contrast is \jdContrast, and across \jdNmonths\ months the contrast scatters on both sides of unity
(median \jdCmMed, range \jdCmMin--\jdCmMax; a one-sample test against unity does not reject, and the
earliest month reached \jdCmMax\ --- one orbit can mislead). Both are consistent with the
canonical regions encoding beaming toward a distant near-equatorial observer and/or genuine
month-to-month variability (Io volcanism, plasma-torus state); neither is separated here, and a
beaming-model fit over the full v02 archive is the scoped follow-on.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Io's control of Jovian DAM \citep{bigg1964} is the textbook example of a satellite--magnetosphere
interaction, and the (CML, Io-phase) occurrence map with its A/B/C/D regions \citep{carr1983,
marques2017} is its canonical representation --- built from decades of \emph{terrestrial}
observations, i.e.\ from a fixed, distant, near-equatorial vantage. Juno's polar, eccentric orbit
breaks every one of those assumptions. The 2025 public release of the calibrated Juno/Waves
flux densities \citep{louis2021} (daily CDFs to 40.5~MHz with per-channel background and sigma)
makes the from-orbit census reproducible with public data alone.

\section{Method}
Each daily CDF is binned to 15~s; a bin is DAM-active when $\ge$10\% of the 3--40.5~MHz channels
exceed the shipped $\mathrm{Background}+5\Sigma$. The absolute scale (V$^2$m$^{-2}$Hz$^{-1}$)
never enters --- an occurrence census needs only detection against the dataset's own statistics.
The sub-Juno CML comes from Horizons (\code{PDObsLon}, observer \code{500@-61}) at 15-min
sampling, unwrap-interpolated; Io phase is the Lieske mean longitude minus CML
\citep{lieske1987}. Occurrence maps carry per-cell exposure, and cells visited $<$3 times are
masked --- a single orbit does not sample the plane uniformly, and hiding that would manufacture
structure. The synthetic recover-a-known drives the same code with real CML/Io rates and
Bernoulli activity concentrated in the canonical boxes: injected contrast 8.75, recovered 7.2.

\section{Results and honest limits}
Figure~\ref{fig:jd} shows the aggregate map. The range-quartile duty cycles
(\jdAqA\%$\rightarrow$\jdAqD\%, near-to-far) fall by a raw factor $\sim$\jdNearFarRaw\ with
Juno--Jupiter distance --- but this is almost all detection sensitivity, not intrinsic occurrence.
DAM power scales as $1/r^2$, so at smaller range the same emission clears the background$+5\sigma$
floor more often; because the DAM SNR distribution is steeply falling across the threshold, a
modest ($\sim$8$\times$ over the $\sim$\jdRangeNear$\rightarrow$\jdRangeFar~$R_J$ quartile spread)
flux change is amplified into the $\sim$\jdNearFarRaw$\times$ occurrence swing. Correcting each
bin's DAM SNR to a common range ($S\to S(r/r_\mathrm{ref})^2$) and re-thresholding collapses the
near/far ratio to \jdNearFarCorr\ (on the same detector the raw ratio is \jdNearFarRawPninety, so
the collapse is the distance correction, not a detector change). The corrected duty cycles are
\jdAqAcorr\%, \jdAqBcorr\%, \jdAqCcorr\%, \jdAqDcorr\% near-to-far --- non-monotonic (the second
quartile exceeds the first), so there is no clean intrinsic trend, and the residual
$\sim$\jdNearFarCorr$\times$ is best read as an upper bound rather than a detection. It is not
cleanly intrinsic either: Juno's polar orbit couples range to magnetic latitude and hence to CMI
beaming geometry. The ``proximity law'' is thus a threshold-amplified inverse-square visibility
effect, with at most a $\sim$\jdNearFarCorr$\times$ residual we cannot separate from geometry. The
canonical Io boxes do not survive aggregation: contrast \jdContrast\ overall (\jdCells\ cells),
per-month median \jdCmMed\ with range \jdCmMin--\jdCmMax\ across \jdNmonths\ months, and no
strengthening with distance in the resolved quartiles (\jdCqA, \jdCqB, \jdCqC, \jdCqD\ from
near to far). Honest limits: (0) the per-month contrasts carry no individual error bars; a one-sample test
against unity does not reject (consistency, not proof), and genuine month-to-month Io-DAM
variability --- Io volcanism and plasma-torus state are published drivers --- is an
unmodelled alternative to geometric scatter; (i) seven months is still sparse and the far
quartiles are activity-starved
(the \jdCqB--\jdCqD\ values carry few active bins); v01 (2017-02) and v02 months are mixed,
benign for occurrence since detection uses each file's own background statistics;
(ii) the fixed 10\%-of-band activity criterion is one choice among several (it suppresses
narrowband interference but dilutes narrowband DAM); (iii) the $1/r^2$ sensitivity null is a
first-order correction --- it divides out the leading inverse-square detection advantage but not
the CMI beaming geometry that co-varies with Juno's range, so the residual \jdNearFarCorr$\times$
is an upper bound, not an intrinsic measurement; (iv) the census does not separate Io-DAM from non-Io-DAM or
kilometric leakage below 3~MHz; (v) the reduced-contrast reading is a consistency statement, not a proof --- a beaming-model fit over the full v02 archive is the definitive test; and the review-caught
convention error is disclosed above because a synthetic round-trip cannot validate frame
conventions, only machinery.
