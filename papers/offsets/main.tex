\title{Reproducing the Radio--Optical Offset Excess of AGN and Its Alignment with the Parsec-Scale
Jet: ICRF3 $\times$ Gaia DR3 $\times$ MOJAVE}

\author[0009-0008-3289-4447]{Joseph Barbere}
\affiliation{Independent researcher}

\begin{abstract}
The VLBI radio position of an active galactic nucleus marks the synchrotron self-absorbed jet base,
while its Gaia optical position can be pulled toward optical jet or host structure; the two do not
coincide. Across the population the \emph{normalised} radio--optical offset
$\xnorm=\sqrt{(\Delta\alpha^*/\sigma_\alpha)^2+(\Delta\delta/\sigma_\delta)^2}$ shows a heavy tail far
beyond the Rayleigh expectation for pure Gaussian astrometric noise --- a well-established result
\citep{mignard2016,petrovkovalev2017,kovalev2017,lindegren2018,plavin2019}. We reproduce it with a
small, tested, CPU-only tool: ICRF3 \citep{icrf3} cross-matched to Gaia DR3 \citep{gaiadr3} via the CDS
X-Match service \citep{cdsxmatch} (\offN\ matched AGN). The median raw offset is \offMedian~mas, and
\offFracTail\% of sources have $\xnorm>3$ --- against a Rayleigh expectation of just \offRayleigh\%, a
\offExcess$\times$ excess. We then test the offset \emph{direction}: cross-matching the \offJetN\ AGN
that also have a MOJAVE \citep{lister2021} parsec-scale jet position angle, the radio--optical offset
aligns with the jet axis (median jet-axis angle \offJetMedAxis$^\circ$ versus $45^\circ$ for random
orientation; \offJetFracAxis\% within $30^\circ$ of the axis versus $33\%$; Kolmogorov--Smirnov
$p=\offJetKsP$ against uniform), predominantly \emph{downstream} (\offJetFracDown\% within $45^\circ$ of
the jet direction versus $25\%$), and the alignment tightens for the significant offsets. This
reproduces \citet{kovalev2017} and \citet{plavin2019}. A reproducibility and tooling contribution --- a
validated offset statistic, the jet-alignment test, and a reproducible matched catalogue --- not a new
discovery.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Gaia and VLBI measure the same AGN to sub-milliarcsecond precision but at different wavelengths, and
their positions differ systematically. \citet{mignard2016} first showed the radio--optical offsets of
ICRF sources exceed what formal errors allow; \citet{petrovkovalev2017} and \citet{kovalev2017}
attributed the excess to optical structure and showed the offsets align preferentially with the
parsec-scale jet. This is now a standard diagnostic of the AGN disk--jet system \citep{plavin2019}. We
ask the weakest, most reproducible version: with the public catalogues and a $\sim$hundred-line tool,
can one recover the offset excess cleanly, and how large is it?

\section{Methods}
\code{offsets} is a pure-NumPy module (on the \code{jansky} helpers; \citealt{jansky}). For each
matched AGN it forms the offset components $\Delta\alpha^*=(\alpha_o-\alpha_r)\cos\delta_r$ and
$\Delta\delta=\delta_o-\delta_r$, the separation, and the position angle, then the normalised offset
\xnorm\ with per-axis errors combined in quadrature,
$\sigma_\alpha^2=\sigma_{\alpha,\mathrm{radio}}^2+\sigma_{\alpha,\mathrm{Gaia}}^2$ (and likewise in
$\delta$). For pure Gaussian astrometric noise \xnorm\ follows a two-dimensional Rayleigh, so
$P(\xnorm>x)=e^{-x^2/2}$ (1.11\% at $x=3$); a heavier observed tail is real structure. Every function
is tested against a synthetic field in which most offsets are Gaussian noise and an injected minority
carry a structural offset, so the suite runs offline.

\section{Data}
ICRF3 \citep{icrf3} (the VLBI reference frame; positions and formal errors at radio wavelengths) is
cross-matched to Gaia DR3 \citep{gaiadr3} through the CDS X-Match service \citep{cdsxmatch} within a
sub-arcsecond radius, taking the Gaia ``ra\_error''/``dec\_error'' as the optical uncertainties. The
match yields \offN\ AGN with both a radio and an optical position. For the jet-alignment test we add a
third catalogue, MOJAVE XVIII \citep{lister2021} (VizieR \code{J/ApJ/923/30}), whose ``mean innermost
jet position angle'' gives the parsec-scale jet direction; it is positionally matched (within $1''$) to
the same AGN, yielding \offJetN\ with a jet PA.

\section{Results}
The median raw radio--optical separation is \offMedian~mas. Crucially, \offFracTail\% of the matched
AGN have a normalised offset $\xnorm>3$, against the Rayleigh expectation of \offRayleigh\% for pure
Gaussian errors --- a factor of \offExcess\ excess (Figure~\ref{fig:xnorm}). The $\xnorm$ distribution
tracks the Rayleigh curve at small $\xnorm$ and departs from it sharply in the tail, exactly the
behaviour reported by \citet{mignard2016} and \citet{lindegren2018}: a population of AGN whose optical
photocentre is displaced from the VLBI core by milliarcsecond-scale structure, well beyond the formal
astrometric errors. The exact $\xnorm>3$ fraction depends on the error model --- Gaia and VLBI formal
errors are mildly underestimated, which inflates $\xnorm$, so \offFracTail\% is an upper bound on the
structurally-offset fraction (structure-only estimates are nearer $\sim$9\%; \citealt{petrovkovalev2017})
--- but the excess over Rayleigh is robust to any plausible error inflation.

\section{The offset direction aligns with the jet}
The magnitude excess shows the offsets are real; their \emph{direction} shows what they trace. Of the
matched AGN, \offJetN\ also appear in MOJAVE \citep{lister2021}, which gives the flux-weighted innermost
jet position angle (measured from the core toward the approaching, downstream side). Comparing the
radio--optical offset PA to the jet PA (Figure~\ref{fig:xnorm}, right), the jet-axis angle ---
$\min(\Delta,180^\circ-\Delta)$, which is $0^\circ$ when the offset lies along the jet axis and would
have a median of $45^\circ$ and a within-$30^\circ$ fraction of $1/3$ if offsets were randomly oriented
--- has median \offJetMedAxis$^\circ$, with \offJetFracAxis\% within $30^\circ$ of the axis; a
Kolmogorov--Smirnov test against the uniform distribution gives $p=\offJetKsP$. The alignment is also
\emph{directional}: \offJetFracDown\% of offsets fall within $45^\circ$ of the downstream jet direction
(random $25\%$), as expected if the VLBI core sits upstream of the optical centroid through opacity
(core-shift) while extended optical jet emission lies downstream. As a consistency check the signal
tightens for the more significant offsets ($X>2$: \offJetNsig\ sources, median \offJetMedAxisSig$^\circ$,
\offJetFracAxisSig\% within $30^\circ$) --- expected, since weak offsets are dominated by astrometric
noise with random PA. The result is unchanged when jets with a poorly defined direction are removed.
This reproduces \citet{kovalev2017} and \citet{plavin2019}.

A few caveats bound the claim. MOJAVE is a flux-limited ($>1.5$~Jy at 15~GHz) sample of strongly
Doppler-boosted blazars with well-defined jets close to the line of sight, so the result applies to that
population and is not a statement about AGN in general. The jet PA is measured at 15~GHz while the radio
position is the ICRF3 S/X core; using the 15~GHz kinematic axis as the jet label is standard, and on
parsec scales the inner jet is essentially straight. And the full-matched-sample test is the primary
one; the $X$-cut rows are a qualitative consistency check (nested subsets, not independent
confirmations).

\section{Discussion and reproducibility}
Both literature results are recovered cleanly from public catalogues with a small tested tool: the
radio--optical offset \emph{excess} (AGN optical positions are displaced from their VLBI cores by real
milliarcsecond structure) and the offset \emph{alignment} with the parsec-scale jet (the displacement
is predominantly downstream along the jet, the disk--jet signature of \citealt{kovalev2017} and
\citealt{plavin2019}). The contribution is reproducibility and tooling --- validated offset and
jet-alignment statistics, a reproducible ICRF3$\times$Gaia$\times$MOJAVE matched catalogue, and the
two-panel figure --- not a new astrophysical claim; the value added over the source papers is a fully
open, end-to-end-reproducible pipeline. The remaining literature extension is to model the disk versus
jet contributions to the optical centroid quantitatively \citep{plavin2019}. Every number above is
written by the pipeline into the macros this paper \code{\textbackslash input}s, and the analysis
regenerates from a clean checkout on public data.
