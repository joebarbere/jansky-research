\title{Reproducing the Radio--Optical Position-Offset Excess of AGN with a Small Tested Tool:
ICRF3 $\times$ Gaia DR3}

\author[0009-0008-3289-4447]{Joseph Barbere}
\affiliation{Independent researcher}

\begin{abstract}
The VLBI radio position of an active galactic nucleus marks the synchrotron self-absorbed jet base,
while its Gaia optical position can be pulled toward optical jet or host structure; the two do not
coincide. Across the population the \emph{normalised} radio--optical offset
$\xnorm=\sqrt{(\Delta\alpha^*/\sigma_\alpha)^2+(\Delta\delta/\sigma_\delta)^2}$ shows a heavy tail far
beyond the Rayleigh expectation for pure Gaussian astrometric noise --- a well-established result
\citep{mignard2016,petrovkovalev2017,kovalev2017,lindegren2018,plavin2019}. We reproduce it with a
small, tested, CPU-only tool: ICRF3 \citep{icrf3} cross-matched to Gaia DR3 \citep{gaiadr3} via the CDS
X-Match service \citep{cdsxmatch} (\offN\ matched AGN). The median raw offset is \offMedian~mas, and
\offFracTail\% of sources have $\xnorm>3$ --- against a Rayleigh expectation of just \offRayleigh\%, a
\offExcess$\times$ excess. The excess is the signature of real milliarcsecond-scale structure, not
astrometric noise. This is a reproducibility and tooling contribution --- a validated offset
statistic and a reproducible matched catalogue --- not a new discovery.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Gaia and VLBI measure the same AGN to sub-milliarcsecond precision but at different wavelengths, and
their positions differ systematically. \citet{mignard2016} first showed the radio--optical offsets of
ICRF sources exceed what formal errors allow; \citet{petrovkovalev2017} and \citet{kovalev2017}
attributed the excess to optical structure and showed the offsets align preferentially with the
parsec-scale jet. This is now a standard diagnostic of the AGN disk--jet system \citep{plavin2019}. We
ask the weakest, most reproducible version: with the public catalogues and a $\sim$hundred-line tool,
can one recover the offset excess cleanly, and how large is it?

\section{Methods}
\code{offsets} is a pure-NumPy module (on the \code{jansky} helpers; \citealt{jansky}). For each
matched AGN it forms the offset components $\Delta\alpha^*=(\alpha_o-\alpha_r)\cos\delta_r$ and
$\Delta\delta=\delta_o-\delta_r$, the separation, and the position angle, then the normalised offset
\xnorm\ with per-axis errors combined in quadrature,
$\sigma_\alpha^2=\sigma_{\alpha,\mathrm{radio}}^2+\sigma_{\alpha,\mathrm{Gaia}}^2$ (and likewise in
$\delta$). For pure Gaussian astrometric noise \xnorm\ follows a two-dimensional Rayleigh, so
$P(\xnorm>x)=e^{-x^2/2}$ (1.11\% at $x=3$); a heavier observed tail is real structure. Every function
is tested against a synthetic field in which most offsets are Gaussian noise and an injected minority
carry a structural offset, so the suite runs offline.

\section{Data}
ICRF3 \citep{icrf3} (the VLBI reference frame; positions and formal errors at radio wavelengths) is
cross-matched to Gaia DR3 \citep{gaiadr3} through the CDS X-Match service \citep{cdsxmatch} within a
sub-arcsecond radius, taking the Gaia ``ra\_error''/``dec\_error'' as the optical uncertainties. The
match yields \offN\ AGN with both a radio and an optical position.

\section{Results}
The median raw radio--optical separation is \offMedian~mas. Crucially, \offFracTail\% of the matched
AGN have a normalised offset $\xnorm>3$, against the Rayleigh expectation of \offRayleigh\% for pure
Gaussian errors --- a factor of \offExcess\ excess (Figure~\ref{fig:xnorm}). The $\xnorm$ distribution
tracks the Rayleigh curve at small $\xnorm$ and departs from it sharply in the tail, exactly the
behaviour reported by \citet{mignard2016} and \citet{lindegren2018}: a population of AGN whose optical
photocentre is displaced from the VLBI core by milliarcsecond-scale structure, well beyond the formal
astrometric errors. The exact $\xnorm>3$ fraction depends on the error model --- Gaia and VLBI formal
errors are mildly underestimated, which inflates $\xnorm$, so \offFracTail\% is an upper bound on the
structurally-offset fraction (structure-only estimates are nearer $\sim$9\%; \citealt{petrovkovalev2017})
--- but the excess over Rayleigh is robust to any plausible error inflation.

\section{Discussion and reproducibility}
The radio--optical offset excess is recovered cleanly from public catalogues with a small tested tool,
reproducing the literature result that AGN optical positions are systematically displaced from their
VLBI cores by real structure. The contribution is reproducibility and tooling --- a validated offset
and normalised-offset statistic, a reproducible matched catalogue, and a clean excess-vs-Rayleigh
figure --- not a new astrophysical claim. The natural extensions, all in the literature, are to
correlate the offset direction with the VLBI jet position angle \citep{kovalev2017} and to model the
disk--jet contributions \citep{plavin2019}; both need VLBI jet PAs beyond this catalogue-only scope.
