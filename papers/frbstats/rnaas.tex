\title{A Lightweight, Reproducible Recovery of the CHIME/FRB Repeater--Width
Difference from the First CHIME/FRB Catalog}

\author[0009-0008-3289-4447]{Joseph Barbere}
\affiliation{Independent researcher}

\begin{abstract}
Using a small, tested, CPU-only tool (\code{frbstats}), I recompute repeater versus
non-repeater burst statistics from the public First CHIME/FRB Catalog and recover the
established result that repeating sources have broader temporal widths than apparent
non-repeaters (Kolmogorov--Smirnov $D=\ksWidthD$, $p=\ksWidthP$). This is a
reproducibility and tooling note, not a new measurement.
\end{abstract}

\section{Result}

Fast-radio-burst population studies routinely recompute wait-time, energy, and
repeater/non-repeater statistics from scratch, with no maintained, installable,
CPU-only library to do so reproducibly. \code{frbstats} \citep{jansky} is such a tool:
a pure-NumPy/SciPy module that loads a public CHIME/FRB catalogue, collapses
multi-component bursts to one row per event, and returns Weibull wait-time fits, a
Clauset--Hill power-law energy fit with a Kolmogorov--Smirnov-selected lower bound,
and two-sample comparisons of the repeater and non-repeater populations, each with
uncertainties. Every routine is tested against a synthetic catalogue with known
ground truth, so the suite runs offline.

Run on the \nBursts\ independent events of the First CHIME/FRB Catalog
\citep{chimecat1} (\nRepeaterBursts\ repeater bursts from \nRepeaterSources\ sources),
the tool recovers the established morphological result that repeater bursts have
significantly larger temporal widths than apparent non-repeaters: a two-sample
Kolmogorov--Smirnov test gives $D=\ksWidthD$ ($p=\ksWidthP$), with median widths
$\medWidthRep$~ms versus $\medWidthOne$~ms (Figure~\ref{fig:dm} shows the companion
dispersion-measure split). This reproduces the peer-reviewed CHIME finding and is the
validation that the implementation is correct. The differential fluence distribution
follows a power law of index $\gamma=\energyGamma\pm\energyGammaErr$ above an
automatically selected completeness bound of $\energyFmin$~Jy~ms, consistent with the
catalogue's published source counts.

The result is presented as a reproduction, not a discovery: the catalogue-level
differences are subject to CHIME's selection function and are not corrected for it,
and within-source wait-time clustering is cadence-dominated for a transit instrument
and is therefore not interpreted here. The full analysis, including this note,
regenerates from a clean checkout with one command, and every number above is
\code{\textbackslash input} from a pipeline-generated macro file, so the text always
matches the pipeline.
