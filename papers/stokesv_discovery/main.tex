\title{Two-Epoch Forced Stokes-V Photometry of the Nearest M Dwarfs in RACS-mid:
Method, a GJ 65 Variability Recovery, and Upper Limits}
\author[0009-0008-3289-4447]{Joseph Barbere}
\affiliation{Independent researcher}

\begin{abstract}
Circular polarization is a near-unambiguous flag of coherent radio emission, but a single survey
epoch undercounts the flaring-emitter population --- an emitter caught between bursts shows
nothing. We present a targeted, reproducible two-epoch Stokes-V forced-photometry pipeline for the
nearest M dwarfs: Gaia proper motions propagate each CNS5 \citep{golovin2023} position to each
observation's epoch (mandatory --- Barnard's star moves $\sim$40$''$ between the epochs used),
CASDA SODA cutouts of RACS-mid \citep{mcconnell2020,hale2021} images at MJD $\approx$59233 and
60769 (a 4.2-yr same-band pair at 1367.5~MHz) yield forced $I$ and $V$ at each propagated
position, and a per-epoch leakage floor guards the killer systematic (off-axis $I\rightarrow V$
leakage). On a synthetic epoch pair with injected steady emitters, single-epoch flares, and
leakage contaminants, the pipeline's selection is complete and pure (\svdComp/\svdPur) and the
two-epoch step flags exactly the flares (completeness \svdVarComp); a fraction \svdMissFrac\ of
injected emitters are invisible to any single epoch. On the real sky (\svdRealN\ nearest M dwarfs
measured; \svdRealPairs\ complete epoch pairs) the pipeline recovers the prototype coherent
emitter --- the GJ 65 system (BL+UV Ceti; also in the RACS-low2 blind V catalogue,
\citealt{racs8_2026}) --- at $V=9.26\rightarrow7.11$~mJy between epochs, a $10\sigma$
inter-epoch change at 1367.5~MHz, while every other target is quiescent with a median $5\sigma$
$V$ limit of \svdRealVlim~mJy. This is a methods + recover-a-known contribution with an
upper-limit census, not a discovery claim.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Blind Stokes-V searches of ASKAP surveys \citep{pritchard2021,driessen2024,racs8_2026} have
established circular polarization as the cleanest wide-field flag of coherent stellar emission.
Two things a blind single-epoch catalogue cannot provide are (i) measurements \emph{below} its
extraction threshold at known stellar positions, and (ii) the time-domain behaviour of the $V$
sky. Our earlier RACS Stokes-V methods work found the honest limit of a single epoch: coherent
emission is bursty, so one epoch undercounts the population. This paper supplies both missing
pieces for the nearest M dwarfs: forced photometry on a curated CNS5 target list, at two RACS-mid
epochs 4.2~yr apart.

\section{Method}
Targets are the nearest CNS5 M dwarfs (red Gaia colour, Dec $<+40^\circ$, sorted by parallax).
Each position is propagated with its Gaia DR3 proper motion to each image's observation MJD ---
at RACS-mid resolution this is not optional: the highest-proper-motion targets move tens of
arcseconds between the epochs. For each target and epoch we stage the RACS-mid \code{taylor.0}
restored $I$ and $V$ images via CASDA SODA, measure the forced peak flux at the propagated pixel
with a local-annulus rms error, and apply the per-epoch leakage floor (7$\times$ the field median
$|V|/I$). Two-epoch variability uses the signed fluxes, $|V_2-V_1|/\sqrt{\sigma_1^2+\sigma_2^2}$,
so a handedness flip registers. The synthetic epoch-pair fixture injects steady emitters,
single-epoch flares, and leakage-only contaminants: selection completeness \svdComp, purity
\svdPur, flare-flagging completeness \svdVarComp\ and purity \svdVarPur, with \svdMissFrac\ of
emitters missed by epoch~1 alone --- the quantified rationale for multi-epoch $V$ work.

\section{Results on the real sky}
Of 60 targets, \svdRealN\ returned measurements in at least one epoch and \svdRealPairs\ have
both (CASDA staging failures account for the rest --- an availability, not detection, effect;
the failure list ships with the reproduction script). \svdRealDet\ target rows exceed $5\sigma$
in $V$: both are the GJ 65 system (BL+UV Ceti, one beam), detected at $V=9.26\pm0.15$~mJy
(MJD 59233) and $7.11\pm0.15$~mJy (MJD 60769) --- deep circular polarization in both epochs and
a $10\sigma$ inter-epoch decline at 1367.5~MHz. GJ 65 is a known radio star (it appears in the
RACS-low2 blind V catalogue, \citealt{racs8_2026}); the recovery validates the pipeline, and the
same-band 4.2-yr $V$ change is the kind of measurement blind single-epoch catalogues do not
provide. Every other measured target is quiescent, with a median $5\sigma$ $V$ upper limit of
\svdRealVlim~mJy --- at 3~pc that is a luminosity limit at or near the lower boundary of known M-dwarf coherent-burst
luminosities ($\sim$10$^{13}$~erg\,s$^{-1}$\,Hz$^{-1}$), i.e.\ consistent with duty cycles well
below unity.

\section{Honest limits}
(i) GJ 65 is a recovery, not a discovery --- every candidate here was cross-checked against the
blind catalogues before the word ``new'' could be used, and none survived that bar. (ii) Two
epochs cannot distinguish a flare from secular change; they bound variability, not its timescale
--- and since UV~Cet-type bursts last minutes to hours against RACS's $\sim$15-min integrations,
both GJ~65 snapshots may themselves be burst states at different levels: the quiescent $V$ floor
of GJ~65 is unconstrained by this dataset.
(iii) staging availability, not detection, limits the census: 35\% of targets returned no data in
any epoch and roughly half of the measured targets lack a complete second epoch --- overall
$\sim$52\% of target-epoch slots failed CASDA staging in this run; the per-row failure accounting
ships with the reproduction script. (iv) The leakage floor is a
per-field statistic; a beam-position-dependent model would sharpen faint candidates. (v) The
quiescent limits apply at the two sampled epochs only --- the M-dwarf $V$ sky remains
undersampled in time, which is precisely the case for multi-epoch forced $V$ monitoring as VAST
and future RACS epochs accumulate.
