\title{Noise-Debiased Rotation-Measure Structure Functions from SPICE-RACS DR1:
Method, Recover-a-Known, and the Full-Sky Disc--Halo Contrast from DR2}
\author[0009-0008-3289-4447]{Joseph Barbere}
\affiliation{Independent researcher}

\begin{abstract}
The SPICE-RACS programme has multiplied the Faraday rotation-measure (RM) grid; its second data
release \citep{spiceracs_dr2} ships $\sim$3.3$\times$10$^5$ quality-flagged (goodRM) components
over 87.5\% of the sky --- released without a systematic Galactic structure-function analysis
(per-region NVSS structure functions exist; \citealt{stil2011}). We supply the reproducible tool
and its validation: a second-order RM structure function, \emph{noise-debiased} by subtracting
the per-pair measurement variance \citep[the convention of][]{haverkorn2004}, with source-level
bootstrap errors and recorded (unique-)pair subsampling. On a synthetic RM screen the pipeline
recovers an injected plane enhancement (\rmsSynRatio$\pm$\rmsSynRatioSe\ for an amplitude boost
of 5) and an injected 2$^\circ$ coherence scale within the Gaussian-ACF factor
$2\sqrt{\ln 2}\approx1.67$ and the log-bin resolution; a pure-noise sky debiases to
$\mathrm{SF}\approx0$. Applied to the DR2 goodRM sample (\rmsRealN\ components at S/N
$\ge 8$ --- matching the release's pre-deduplication goodRM count to one row), the latitude
enhancement ratio is \rmsRealRatio$\pm$\rmsRealRatioSe\ (bootstrap-statistical error only), the
disc/halo SF plateaus are \rmsRealPlatLo/\rmsRealPlatHi~rad$^2$\,m$^{-4}$, and a six-bin
latitude ladder falls monotonically from $\sigma_\mathrm{RM}=\rmsRealSigPlane$ to
\rmsRealSigPole~rad\,m$^{-2}$ --- the polar endpoint at the literature intrinsic+extragalactic
floor, licensing a first-order quadrature floor subtraction. Each plateau individually is an
upper bound (source-intrinsic scatter unremoved; the disc bins additionally mix secular plane
gradients into the large-separation SF), but the floor is latitude-independent, so the
disc--halo \emph{difference} and the ladder's \emph{shape} are dominated by the Galactic
magneto-ionic medium. Applying the survey's own quality flags proved material: without them the
sample is leakage-contaminated and the high-latitude break scale is spuriously small.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
RM grids sample the integral $\int n_e B_\parallel\,dl$ along every extragalactic sightline; the
\emph{spatial structure} of the RM sky --- how RM decorrelates with angular separation --- encodes
the outer scale and amplitude of magneto-ionic turbulence \citep{haverkorn2004}. Our earlier
slice recovered the Taylor et al. (2009) latitude profile and quadrant signs from 37,543 NVSS RMs
\citep{taylor2009}; SPICE-RACS raises the areal density to 6.7~deg$^{-2}$
\citep{spiceracs_dr2}, making per-latitude-bin structure functions newly measurable. The DR2
release paper provides the catalogue but not this analysis; we build the reproducible tool.

\section{Method and recover-a-known}
For all source pairs in angular-separation bins,
$\mathrm{SF}(\delta\theta)=\langle(\mathrm{RM}_1-\mathrm{RM}_2)^2\rangle-\langle\sigma_1^2+
\sigma_2^2\rangle$: the second term removes the measurement-noise floor that otherwise dominates
faint-grid SFs. Errors come from bootstrap resampling of \emph{sources} (pairs are correlated);
pair counts are capped with the subsample fraction recorded. Validation: (i) a pure-noise sky
debiases to $\mathrm{SF}\approx0$; (ii) a synthetic Gaussian-blob screen with a 2$^\circ$
coherence scale and a 5$\times$ plane amplitude boost returns an enhancement ratio of
\rmsSynRatio$\pm$\rmsSynRatioSe, a low-$|b|$/high-$|b|$ SF plateau ratio $\gg$1
(fixture values in the \code{rmsSyn} macro namespace), and a half-plateau break
consistent with the injected scale times the Gaussian-ACF factor $2\sqrt{\ln 2}\approx1.67$
(the binned estimator resolves this only to the log-bin width, $\sim$30\% at that scale).

\section{The real legs: DR1, then the full-sky DR2 goodRM sample}
The bounded first leg used the DR1 catalogue on CASDA TAP; its raw S/N cut (7{,}707 rows)
exceeds the DR1 paper's fully-flagged 5{,}818 RM measurements because the leakage and
Stokes-I-fit flags were not applied --- a lesson promoted to the main result: the DR2 leg uses
the release's own \code{goodRM} flag, and the resulting \rmsRealN\ components match the DR2
paper's pre-deduplication goodRM count to one row (field-overlap duplicates sit at arcsecond
separations, below our smallest SF bin, so they do not enter any pair statistic). On this
sample the latitude enhancement ratio is \rmsRealRatio$\pm$\rmsRealRatioSe\ --- roughly double
the 5.4 we recover from Taylor et al.\ (2009) with the same statistic. That comparison is not
apples-to-apples and the direction of both dominant differences is expected to raise the
SPICE-RACS value: NVSS's two-band $n\pi$ ambiguity caps large plane $|\mathrm{RM}|$ (deflating
its plane median), and the southern coverage adds inner-Galaxy sightlines; the quoted error is
bootstrap-statistical only, with these systematics dominating. The disc/halo SF plateaus are
\rmsRealPlatLo\ and \rmsRealPlatHi~rad$^2$\,m$^{-4}$, and the six-bin ladder gives
$\sigma_\mathrm{RM}$ falling monotonically from \rmsRealSigPlane\ ($|b|<5^\circ$) to
\rmsRealSigPole~rad\,m$^{-2}$ ($|b|>50^\circ$) --- a factor $\sim$18 profile whose polar
endpoint sits at the literature floor \citep{mao2010,taylor2009}, licensing the quadrature
floor subtraction for the Galactic-only shape. Quality flags proved material beyond counts: on
the unflagged sample the high-latitude half-plateau scale is spuriously small
(0.5$^\circ$ vs \rmsRealBreakHi$^\circ$ flagged) --- leakage-contaminated sources masquerade
as small-scale RM structure.

\section{Honest limits}
(i) Each SF plateau is an upper bound on the Galactic term: source-intrinsic + extragalactic RM
scatter is unremoved, and in the disc bins the large-separation SF additionally mixes secular
plane gradients (ordered-field structure over tens of degrees), so the plane
$\sigma_\mathrm{RM}$ is an effective, not purely turbulent, amplitude. (ii) The floor
subtraction treats the polar bin as pure floor --- any true polar Galactic term biases the
subtracted profile low near the pole. (iii) The Taylor et al.\ (2009) ratio comparison carries
frequency, $n\pi$-handling, and sky-coverage systematics (Section 3) that dominate the quoted
statistical errors. (iv) The half-plateau break statistic is bin-width-limited ($\sim$30\% at
these scales) and validated only against the fixture's known scale, not a turbulence model.
(v) Bootstrap errors use a unique-set source resample (conservative by $\sim$30\%); pair
fractions are quoted per unique pair (ordered draws double-count). (vi) $n\pi$ resolution is
inherited from the upstream RM-synthesis pipeline. (vii) At DR2 scale pairs are randomly
sampled rather than exhausted --- an unbiased estimator of the bin means, with the sampled
fraction recorded.
