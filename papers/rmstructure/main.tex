\title{Noise-Debiased Rotation-Measure Structure Functions from SPICE-RACS DR1:
Method, Recover-a-Known, and a First High-Latitude Coherence Measurement}
\author[0009-0008-3289-4447]{Joseph Barbere}
\affiliation{Independent researcher}

\begin{abstract}
The SPICE-RACS programme has multiplied the Faraday rotation-measure (RM) grid: DR1 provides
24,758 RMs over $\sim$1300~deg$^2$ \citep{thomson2023} and DR2 $\sim$2.5--3.4$\times$10$^5$ over
87.5\% of the sky \citep{spiceracs_dr2} --- released without a systematic Galactic
structure-function analysis. We supply the tooling and its validation: a second-order RM
structure function, \emph{noise-debiased} by subtracting the per-pair measurement variance
\citep[the convention of][]{haverkorn2004}, with source-level bootstrap errors and recorded pair
subsampling. On a synthetic RM screen with a known coherence scale and a known plane enhancement
the pipeline recovers both (enhancement ratio \rmsRatio$\pm$\rmsRatioSe\ for an injected
amplitude boost of 5; SF break tracking the injected 2$^\circ$ scale within the Gaussian-ACF
geometric factor), and a pure-noise sky debiases to SF $\approx 0$ rather than the
$2\sigma^2$ floor. Applied to the public DR1 catalogue (\rmsN\ RMs at S/N $\ge 8$, CASDA TAP, no
auth), the high-latitude structure function plateaus at \rmsPlatHi~rad$^2$\,m$^{-4}$ with a
half-plateau scale of \rmsBreakHi$^\circ$. DR1's catalogue excludes the Galactic plane, so the
disc--halo comparison this method is built for awaits the DR2 table (public, 4.97~GB, CSIRO DAP)
--- this paper validates the method and stakes the bounded first measurement, not the full
census.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
RM grids sample the integral $\int n_e B_\parallel\,dl$ along every extragalactic sightline; the
\emph{spatial structure} of the RM sky --- how RM decorrelates with angular separation --- encodes
the outer scale and amplitude of magneto-ionic turbulence \citep{haverkorn2004}. Our earlier
slice recovered the Taylor et al. (2009) latitude profile and quadrant signs from 37,543 NVSS RMs
\citep{taylor2009}; SPICE-RACS raises the areal density to 6.7~deg$^{-2}$
\citep{spiceracs_dr2}, making per-latitude-bin structure functions newly measurable. The DR2
release paper provides the catalogue but not this analysis; we build the reproducible tool.

\section{Method and recover-a-known}
For all source pairs in angular-separation bins,
$\mathrm{SF}(\delta\theta)=\langle(\mathrm{RM}_1-\mathrm{RM}_2)^2\rangle-\langle\sigma_1^2+
\sigma_2^2\rangle$: the second term removes the measurement-noise floor that otherwise dominates
faint-grid SFs. Errors come from bootstrap resampling of \emph{sources} (pairs are correlated);
pair counts are capped with the subsample fraction recorded. Validation: (i) a pure-noise sky
debiases to $\mathrm{SF}\approx0$; (ii) a synthetic Gaussian-blob screen with a 2$^\circ$
coherence scale and a 5$\times$ plane amplitude boost returns an enhancement ratio of
\rmsRatio$\pm$\rmsRatioSe, a low-$|b|$/high-$|b|$ SF plateau ratio $\gg$1
(\rmsPlatLo\ vs \rmsPlatHi\ rad$^2$\,m$^{-4}$ in the fixture), and a half-plateau break
consistent with the injected scale times the Gaussian-ACF factor ($\approx$1.7).

\section{First real leg: SPICE-RACS DR1}
The DR1 catalogue is queryable unauthenticated from CASDA TAP
(\code{AS110.spice\_racs\_dr1\_corrected\_cut\_v02}; 24,758 rows verified). At S/N $\ge 8$
(\rmsN\ RMs) the high-latitude structure function plateaus at \rmsPlatHi~rad$^2$\,m$^{-4}$
(RM dispersion $\sim\sqrt{\mathrm{SF}/2}\approx54$~rad\,m$^{-2}$ over the DR1 footprint) with a
half-plateau scale of \rmsBreakHi$^\circ$. Two honest qualifications frame this number: the DR1
\emph{corrected-cut} catalogue excludes the Galactic plane, so the low-$|b|$ column of the method
is empty at DR1 (the disc--halo comparison awaits DR2); and the plateau mixes Galactic
fluctuation power with the intrinsic + extragalactic RM scatter of the background sources, so it
is an upper bound on the Galactic term at these latitudes.

\section{Honest limits}
(i) DR1 has no plane coverage --- the headline disc--halo SF contrast is validated on synthetics
only until the DR2 run. (ii) The SF plateau includes intrinsic/extragalactic RM scatter
(tens of rad$^2$\,m$^{-4}$); without a compact-double or DEFROST-style separation the Galactic
amplitude is an upper bound. (iii) The two-band nature of RM catalogues leaves $n\pi$ ambiguity
to the upstream pipeline; we inherit SPICE-RACS' resolution of it. (iv) The half-plateau break
statistic is a crude scale estimator, validated against the fixture's known scale, not a fitted
turbulence model.
