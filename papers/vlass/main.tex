\title{A Reproducible CPU-Only Pipeline for VLASS Multi-Epoch Radio Variability:
Quick-Look Systematics, a \vlassArea\,deg$^2$ Census, and the Recovery of FK~Comae~Berenices}

\author[0009-0008-3289-4447]{Joseph Barbere}
\affiliation{Independent researcher}

\begin{abstract}
The Karl G.\ Jansky Very Large Array Sky Survey \citep[VLASS;][]{lacy2020} imaged the sky north of
$\delta=-40\arcdeg$ at 2--4~GHz in three epochs (2017--2024), making it a natural hunting ground for
radio variability. We ask how much of that variability is accessible from the public Quick-Look (QL)
component catalogues alone, with a small, tested, CPU-only tool. Cross-matching the three QL epochs
and selecting outliers in the standard variability metrics $\eta$ (significance) and $V$ (amplitude)
turns out to be \emph{dominated by source-extraction artefacts}: component deblending, cross-match
incompleteness, and bright-neighbour confusion. We show that three mitigations are necessary and
sufficient to obtain a believable candidate list --- a per-epoch QL flux-scale correction
\citep{vlassmemo13, vlassmemo22}, a deblending isolation cut, and automatic \emph{forced-photometry
confirmation} against the actual images with a peak-centring gate. Over a \vlassArea~deg$^2$ census
(\vlassNsources\ sources, \vlassNusable\ usable), the $\vlassSigma\sigma$ selection yields
\vlassNcand\ candidates, of which \vlassNconfirmed\ survive image confirmation; one is the known
rapidly rotating active giant \emph{FK~Comae~Berenices}, recovered with a clean $\sim$7$\to$1~mJy
decline at a $0.9\arcsec$ match. Injection--recovery shows the three-epoch selection saturates near
\vlassComplMax\% completeness even for extreme flares (50\% at $\sim\vlassComplFifty\times$), so the
confirmed sources are a floor, not a rate. This is a tooling and methodology contribution --- a
validation (FK~Com) plus a completeness-bounded limit --- not a discovery.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Repeated all-sky imaging makes radio variability a survey science: a source seen at several epochs
yields a light curve, and outliers flag flaring AGN, radio stars, and slow transients. VLASS
\citep{lacy2020} provides three 2--4~GHz epochs over $33{,}885$~deg$^2$, and its Quick-Look component
catalogues \citep{gordon2021} are public. We test the weakest, most reproducible version of the
problem: with a $\sim$few-hundred-line CPU-only tool and the public catalogues alone, how many
genuine variables can one recover, and how badly do the QL systematics bite? The honest answer is
both cautionary and positive.

\section{Methods}
\code{vlass} is a pure-NumPy/SciPy/astropy module (on the \code{jansky} helpers; \citealt{jansky}).
For each source it builds a per-epoch light curve and computes the two transient-survey metrics
\citep{swinbank2015, rowlinson2019}: the weighted reduced $\chi^2$ against a constant flux,
$\eta = \frac{1}{N-1}\sum_i (S_i-\bar S_w)^2/\sigma_i^2$ (significance of variability), and the
coefficient of variation $V=\sigma_S/\bar S$ (amplitude). Sources that are outliers in \emph{both}
$\log\eta$ and $\log V$ at $\vlassSigma\sigma$ are candidates. Three mitigations make the result
trustworthy:

\paragraph{Per-epoch flux scale.} The QL images underestimate peak flux, epoch-dependently
(Epoch~1 $\sim$15\%, Epoch~2 $\sim$7\%, Epoch~3 $\sim$3\%; \citealt{vlassmemo13, vlassmemo22}). We
correct each epoch to the Perley--Butler scale and fold the $\sim$7\% residual scatter into the
errors; without this the epoch-to-epoch offset alone flags every source.

\paragraph{Deblending isolation.} A slightly extended source is fit with a different number of
Gaussian components in each epoch, so a secondary component's flux jumps around. We drop sources with
a catalogue neighbour within $5\arcsec$.

\paragraph{Forced-photometry confirmation.} Every catalogue candidate is checked against the images:
we perform forced peak photometry at the locked position in each epoch's QL cutout (one CADC query
per source), recompute $\eta$/$V$ on the \emph{image} light curve, and confirm a candidate only if it
is significantly variable (p$<0.01$) with real amplitude ($V>0.3$) \emph{and} the forced peak in the
brightest epoch is centred within $2.5\arcsec$. The centring gate is essential: a bright source just
outside the locked position otherwise pins the forced peak at the search-box edge and fakes a
variable as its wings shift between epochs.

Selection completeness is measured by injection--recovery on the real data: a single-epoch flare of
known factor is injected into the steady light curves and the field's own cut is re-run. Every
function is tested against a synthetic three-epoch population, so the suite runs offline.

\section{Data}
We use the CIRADA Epoch~1--2 and NRAO Epoch~3 QL component catalogues \citep{gordon2021,vlassmemo22}
(Epoch~1 via VizieR TAP, Epochs~2--3 via the bulk NRAO files), cross-matched within $2.5\arcsec$, and
the CADC Quick-Look image cutouts for forced photometry. A subtle trap: the VizieR Epoch-1 peak flux
is recorded in $\mu$Jy while mislabelled ``mJy/beam'' --- a factor of $10^3$ that, uncaught, makes
every source appear variable; we detect it from the catalogue flux floor (the $\sim$0.7~mJy detection
threshold) and rescale.

\section{Results}
Over a \vlassArea~deg$^2$ cone (\vlassNsources\ sources; \vlassNexcluded\ dropped as crowded;
\vlassNusable\ usable), the selection flags \vlassNcand\ candidates and image confirmation keeps
\vlassNconfirmed.

\paragraph{A real recovery.} One confirmed source matches \emph{FK~Comae~Berenices} at $0.9\arcsec$,
the prototype of the FK~Com class of rapidly rotating, magnetically active single giants and a known
coronally active radio emitter \citep{gudel2002}. Its forced light curve declines monotonically
($\sim$7.2, 2.7, 1.0~mJy/beam across the three epochs; upper track of Figure~\ref{fig:fkcom}),
confirmed in both the catalogue and the images. Recovering it purely from public catalogues is the validation that the
pipeline surfaces genuine variables, not only rejects artefacts. The second survivor is a faint
($\sim$3~mJy) centred source fading below threshold, with no catalogued counterpart --- a plausible
transient reported as a candidate, not a detection. Several other candidates carry blazar/QSO/BL-Lac
identifications but fall \emph{below} the $V>0.3$ bar, i.e.\ real AGN varying at the few-tens-of-
percent level the cadence expects, correctly not over-claimed.

\paragraph{Completeness and the three-epoch ceiling.} Injection--recovery (Figure~\ref{fig:compl})
shows the selection recovers $<$5\% of $\le$3$\times$ single-epoch flares and \emph{saturates near
\vlassComplMax\%} even at $10\times$ (50\% at $\sim\vlassComplFifty\times$). This is intrinsic to
three epochs: a single-epoch flare's coefficient of variation tops out at $1/\sqrt{3}/(1/3)=1.73$ as
the amplitude $\to\infty$, just above the noisy three-epoch $V$ threshold. The \vlassNconfirmed\
confirmed sources are therefore a floor; the strong-single-epoch-variable fraction is of order a few
$\times10^{-4}$.

\section{Discussion and reproducibility}
Catalogue-only VLASS QL multi-epoch variability selection is dominated by source-extraction artefacts,
and every statistical candidate must be confirmed against the images before it is believed --- the
cautionary thread shared with much survey transient work. But the same pipeline, given enough sky,
also does its positive job: it recovers a genuine variable (FK~Com) and sets an honest,
completeness-bounded floor on the rest. The contribution is the reproducible, QL-systematic-aware
tool --- flux-scale correction, deblending isolation, centred forced-photometry confirmation, and
data-driven completeness --- not a discovery. Every census number above is written by the pipeline
into the macros this paper \code{\textbackslash input}s, and the analysis regenerates from a clean
checkout. A looser catalogue cut (viable because forced photometry removes false positives), more
sky, or per-epoch forced photometry of every source would tighten the limit.
