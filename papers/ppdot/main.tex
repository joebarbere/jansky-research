\title{The Pulsar P--$\dot P$ Diagram, Surface Fields, and the Death Line:
A Reproducible Recovery from the ATNF Catalogue}

\author[0009-0008-3289-4447]{Joseph Barbere}
\affiliation{Independent researcher}

\begin{abstract}
A rotation-powered pulsar's period $P$ and spin-down rate $\dot P$ fix its surface dipole field
$B\approx3.2\times10^{19}\sqrt{P\dot P}$ G, characteristic age $\tau=P/2\dot P$, and spin-down
luminosity $\dot E=4\pi^2 I\dot P/P^3$. The $P$--$\dot P$ plane is the H--R diagram of pulsars. We build
a small tested tool that recovers its population structure from the ATNF Pulsar Catalogue
\citep{manchester2005} (VizieR, public, no auth): of the catalogue, \ppN\ pulsars have a positive
measured $\dot P$ and enter the diagram. Their median surface field separates the three populations by
$\sim$5 orders of magnitude --- millisecond pulsars at $\log(B/\mathrm{G})=\ppLogBmsp$ (recycled), the
ordinary population at \ppLogBnormal, and high-field/magnetar objects ($B>10^{13}$ G) at
\ppLogBmagnetar\ --- and \ppFracAlive\ of the sample lies above a standard polar-cap death line, as
expected for a radio-selected catalogue. The Crab pulsar validates the derivations ($B=3.8\times10^{12}$
G, $\tau\approx1260$ yr). This is a reproducibility/method contribution and a recover-a-known, not a
discovery, and we are explicit about the catalogue selection, the death-line model dependence, and what
$B$ and $\tau$ do and do not mean.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Pulsar spin-down is the cleanest clock in astrophysics, and the period--period-derivative diagram
organises the whole population: ordinary pulsars at $B\sim10^{12}$ G and $P\sim0.1$--1 s, millisecond
pulsars recycled to the bottom-left by binary accretion, magnetars at the top-right, and a death line
below which the polar-cap voltage can no longer sustain the pair production that powers radio emission
\citep{rudermansutherland1975,bhattacharya1991,lorimerkramer2004}. We ask the most reproducible version:
with the public catalogue and a small tested tool, can that structure be recovered, with the right
caveats?

\section{Methods}
\code{ppdot} is a pure-NumPy module on the \code{jansky} helpers \citep{jansky}, reusing the project's
ATNF fetch pattern and its millisecond-pulsar period cut. From $P$ and $\dot P$ it computes the surface
dipole field $B=3.2\times10^{19}\sqrt{P\dot P}$ G --- the standard orthogonal-rotator estimate
($\sin\alpha=1$, $I=10^{45}$ g cm$^2$, $R=10$ km; the aligned/equatorial convention gives
$6.4\times10^{19}$, i.e.\ fields larger by $\sqrt2$) --- the characteristic age $\tau=P/2\dot P$, and the
spin-down luminosity. It labels each pulsar magnetar/high-$B$ ($B>10^{13}$ G), millisecond ($P<30$ ms),
or normal, in that priority, and evaluates a constant-$B/P^2$ death line ($B_{12}/P^2=0.2$;
\citealt{rudermansutherland1975,bhattacharya1991}). Every function is tested against a synthetic
three-population fixture; that round-trip checks the classifier's arithmetic on injected labels and is
\emph{not} a validation of the real results.

\section{Data}
We query the ATNF Pulsar Catalogue \citep{manchester2005} from VizieR (\code{B/psr}): periods and
period derivatives for the known pulsars. Of the $\sim$3500 entries, \ppN\ have a positive measured
$\dot P$ and enter the diagram; the rest lack a measured $\dot P$ or (for many globular-cluster pulsars)
show a negative apparent $\dot P$ from cluster acceleration rather than true spin-down, and are dropped.

\section{Results}
The median surface field cleanly separates the three populations (Figure~\ref{fig:ppdot}):
$\log(B/\mathrm{G}) = \ppLogBmsp$ for the \ppNmsp\ millisecond pulsars, \ppLogBnormal\ for the \ppNnormal\
normal pulsars, and \ppLogBmagnetar\ for the \ppNmagnetar\ high-$B$/magnetar objects --- a span of
$\sim$5 orders of magnitude (a factor of $\sim$80\,000), the textbook P--$\dot P$ structure
\citep{lorimerkramer2004}. The millisecond population sits at the bottom-left (short $P$, tiny $\dot P$,
recycled by accretion); the high-$B$ objects at the top-right. Against the standard polar-cap death
line, \ppFracAlive\ of the sample is radio-loud, as expected for a radio-selected catalogue. The Crab
pulsar validates the derivations: $B=3.8\times10^{12}$ G and $\tau=P/2\dot P\approx1260$ yr, both
textbook (and $\tau$ is appropriately longer than the true $\sim$970-yr age, since $\tau$ assumes
braking index 3 and $P_0\ll P$).

\section{Discussion and reproducibility}
The contribution is method and reproducibility: a tested tool that recovers the P--$\dot P$ population
structure from a public catalogue, with the caveats that bound it. (i) ``Magnetar'' here is a
\emph{position-in-diagram} label ($B>10^{13}$ G; \ppNmagnetar\ sources) that includes high-field
rotation-powered pulsars as well as the $\sim$30 true magnetars --- which are X-ray/$\gamma$-ray, not
radio, selected. (ii) The death line is one representative model; the literature spans a ``death
valley'' \citep{chenruderman1993}, and the $\sim$1.8\% below our line are not noise but real
long-period pulsars that motivated that literature, so \ppFracAlive\ should be read as ``almost all, for
a standard line.'' (iii) The ATNF catalogue is the union of many flux- and period-limited surveys, so
the \emph{relative} population sizes are survey-shaped, not intrinsic, though the per-class median
fields are robust to this. None of these undermine the recover-a-known --- the field separation and the
diagram structure are unambiguous --- but they are why this is a reproduction, not a population
measurement. Every number above is written by the pipeline into the macros this paper
\code{\textbackslash input}s, and \code{python -m jansky\_research.ppdot} regenerates them from the
public catalogue.
