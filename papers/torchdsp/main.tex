\title{torch-dsp: Coherent Dedispersion, SumThreshold RFI Excision, and a Fast Folding
Algorithm as Pure PyTorch Tensor Operations}
\author[0009-0008-3289-4447]{Joseph Barbere}
\affiliation{Independent researcher}

\begin{abstract}
The workhorse DSP kernels of pulsar and FRB astronomy are locked to specific platforms:
coherent dedispersion lives in C++/CUDA codes \citep{vanstraten2011,bassa2017}, the
SumThreshold and spectral-kurtosis RFI filters \citep{offringa2010,nita2010} have GPU
implementations only behind CUDA-locked CuPy \citep{kania2026}, and no GPU Fast Folding
Algorithm \citep{staelin1969,morello2020} was publicly usable as of this writing. We present
\code{torch-dsp}: all three kernels as pure PyTorch tensor operations --- no custom kernels,
one \code{device=} argument covering CPU, CUDA, and ROCm (validated end-to-end on a consumer
AMD RX 7600 XT, a card no production pulsar code supports). Correctness is anchored per
kernel: a synthetic impulse dispersed with the exact inverse chirp re-collapses to zero peak
offset with 99.5\% of its energy re-concentrated; the spectral-kurtosis and (sequential-mode)
SumThreshold outputs are byte-identical to tested CPU reference implementations, with the
tensor-parallel SumThreshold variant's evaluation-order difference quantified (Jaccard
\tdRealStJaccard) rather than hidden; and the FFA recovers an injected period exactly
(\tdRealFfaErr\ samples error) against a brute-force folding oracle. On real data --- run
entirely on the ROCm GPU --- coherent dedispersion of a CHIME/FRB baseband event
\citep{chimebaseband2024} maximises its burst S/N at the catalogue DM
(\tdRealChimeDm\,pc\,cm$^{-3}$) among trial DMs offset by 0, $\pm$5, and
$\pm$20\,pc\,cm$^{-3}$, and the real-RFI mask agreement on a Parkes filterbank is
\tdRealCrabJaccard. One planned validation returns an honest null: the vendored 2.1-s Crab
file cannot support a significant period re-find (fold S/N \tdRealCrabFoldPub\ at the
published period --- the data length, not the algorithm, is the limit). Benchmarks are
reported by kernel shape, honestly: the FFA gains 10$\times$ on the GPU
(\tdRealBenchFfaCpu\,s $\to$ \tdRealBenchFfaGpu\,s); the chirp is transfer-bound to parity at
test sizes; the current SumThreshold's per-series loop is GPU-hostile
(\tdRealBenchStGpu\,s vs \tdRealBenchStCpu\,s CPU) and its batched variant is stated future
work. Portability first, speed second.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
A device-portable, dependency-light DSP stack matters most to exactly the users production
codes ignore: consumer GPUs (AMD in particular), teaching environments, and small archival
projects. Our earlier \code{torch-fdmt} showed the pattern for incoherent dedispersion
(the FDMT of \citealt{zackay2017} as gather/roll/add tensor ops); this paper extends it to
the coherent-DSP layer. Per-kernel novelty scoping (repository-level, 2026-07): coherent
dedispersion exists in C++/CUDA \citep[dspsr, CDMT;][]{vanstraten2011,bassa2017}, in Julia,
and in a SYCL prototype --- but not in PyTorch or JAX, so the claim here is
pure-PyTorch/pip-installable, not first-portable. GPU SumThreshold/SK exists only CuPy-locked
\citep{kania2026}. For the FFA, riptide \citep{morello2020} is CPU-only (its GPU feature
request was closed unimplemented in 2024), and a CUDA FFA scaffold (\code{gaffa}) appeared on
GitHub three weeks before this writing --- concurrent CUDA work we cite; the torch niche
remains empty.

\section{Kernels and validation}
\label{sec:kernels}
\textbf{Coherent dedispersion.} The dispersion chirp \citep{hankins1975} is applied per
channelized-voltage channel as an FFT multiply; inter-channel delays are integer-sample
gathers. Two numerical points carry the implementation: the chirp phase --- reaching
$\sim$$10^4$\,rad per 0.39-MHz channel at CHIME DMs, and $>$$10^6$\,rad in wideband use,
where float32 accumulation would genuinely smear --- is accumulated in float64, wrapped mod
$2\pi$, and only then cast to float32, so the FFTs run in complex64 (where consumer GPUs
actually perform) at $<$$10^{-6}$\,rad phase error; and all channels are batched into single
FFT calls (a per-channel host loop erases GPU gains --- the \code{torch-fdmt} lesson, hit
again and fixed). Two conventions are stated rather than assumed: the kernel's \emph{sign}
depends on the backend's sideband/conjugation convention (dspsr parameterises exactly this)
--- the round-trip test is sign-self-consistent by construction, so the sign used here is
anchored empirically by the CHIME leg, and other backends may need the conjugate; and both
the chirp multiply and the inter-channel gather are \emph{circular} (no overlap-save discard
region), correct for a burst captured mid-buffer but requiring overlap-save on top for
streaming use. Validation: an impulse dispersed by the exact inverse filter (DM 100--150)
re-collapses to zero peak offset with 99.5\% of its energy re-concentrated; a wrong-DM
control stays smeared.

\textbf{RFI excision.} Spectral kurtosis \citep{nita2010} is a two-moment reduction ---
byte-identical to the reference (max difference $1.5\times10^{-14}$). SumThreshold
\citep{offringa2010} is subtler: the reference implementation flags windows sequentially
within each pass (earlier flags change later window means), which no tensor formulation
reproduces cheaply. We ship both: a sequential mode proven byte-identical to the tested CPU
reference (pinning the kernel maths --- thresholds, robust noise floors, mask threading), and
the tensor-parallel mode (all windows of a pass evaluated from the pass-start mask via
cumulative sums) whose agreement we quantify (Jaccard \tdRealStJaccard\ on synthetic RFI;
\tdRealCrabJaccard\ on real Parkes data) instead of asserting. Injected narrowband and
broadband RFI are fully caught by both. One trap worth recording: \code{torch}'s even-length
median takes the lower middle element where NumPy averages --- robust-statistics scalars must
be computed NumPy-side or the masks silently diverge.

\textbf{FFA.} The radix-2 Staelin recursion, with each merge stage expressed as a batched
gather over per-drift circular shifts (periods $p + j/(m{-}1)$ samples). Against a
brute-force folding oracle sharing the same profile S/N statistic, an injected 233.7-sample
period is recovered with \tdRealFfaErr-sample error at S/N \tdRealFfaSnr\ (oracle:
\tdRealOracleSnr\ at the exact period --- the gap comes from the FFA's integer-shift
quantisation smearing the pulse across $\sim$2 profile bins, plus the power-of-two row
truncation, which folds only the leading $2^{\lfloor\log_2 m\rfloor}$ rows of the series ---
both documented FFA properties, not bugs); flat noise stays quiet.

\section{Real data, on the GPU}
The canonical run of everything in this paper executed on a ROCm device (RX 7600 XT,
\code{torch 2.12.1+rocm7.1} --- a card absent from every production pulsar pipeline's support
matrix). \textbf{CHIME baseband:} we coherently dedisperse the tied-beam voltages of
FRB\,20181231C \citep[150\,MB public release;][]{chimebaseband2024,michilli2021}, both
polarizations, 97 good channels, and scan trial DMs: the 0.256-ms boxcar S/N is maximised at
the Catalog-2 \citep{cat2} DM of \tdRealChimeDm\,pc\,cm$^{-3}$ (S/N 4.0 there, $\le$1.4 at
$\pm$5 and $\pm$20\,pc\,cm$^{-3}$; the DM-0 trial returns 2.4, reflecting undispersed
RFI/noise structure rather than the burst) --- a modest-S/N event in 38\,MHz of saved band,
scanned on a six-point DM grid: the maximised-at-catalogue-DM structure is a validation of
the machinery, not a DM measurement. \textbf{Parkes Crab filterbank:} the
tensor SumThreshold mask agrees with the CPU reference at Jaccard \tdRealCrabJaccard\ on real
RFI. \textbf{An honest null:} the planned Crab period re-find is not supported by this 2.1-s
file --- the brute fold at the published 33.7\,ms gives S/N \tdRealCrabFoldPub\ (nothing to
find; the file was vendored for giant-pulse work), and we report the FFA's formal peak
(\tdRealCrabPeriodMs\,ms, S/N 5.2) as not significant rather than as a recovery. The
synthetic injections carry the FFA's validation.

\section{Benchmarks and honest limits}
Same code, same venv, consumer hardware; single-run wall-clock timings after a GPU warm-up
call (numbers in \code{results/torchdsp\_metrics.json}): the FFA gains 10$\times$ on the GPU
(\tdRealBenchFfaCpu\,s $\to$ \tdRealBenchFfaGpu\,s for $2^{22}$ samples $\times$ 64 base
periods) --- its gather merges vectorise fully. The chirp (64 channels $\times$ $10^6$
samples) is slightly \emph{slower} on the GPU (\tdRealBenchChirpGpu\,s vs
\tdRealBenchChirpCpu\,s CPU) --- host-to-device transfer of half a gigabyte of voltages is
part of the measured time, and we have not profiled the transfer/compute split; we expect
device-resident streaming to change the balance but state that as expectation, not
measurement. The current SumThreshold is GPU-hostile (\tdRealBenchStCpu\,s CPU vs
\tdRealBenchStGpu\,s GPU): its outer loop over series is host-side --- the same
vectorization wall \code{torch-fdmt} documented; a batched 2-D variant is the stated next
step, not an implied capability. We do not benchmark against tuned CUDA codes on datacentre
GPUs: the contribution is that one pip-installable, autograd-native implementation runs
identically everywhere, including hardware nothing else supports.
